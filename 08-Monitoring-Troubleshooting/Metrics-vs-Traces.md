One of the most common DevOps, SRE, and Observability interview questions is:

# Interview Question

## Difference Between Metrics, Logs, and Traces

### Answer

Metrics are numerical measurements used to monitor system health and performance. Logs are detailed records of events generated by applications and infrastructure. Traces track the complete path of a request across services, helping identify latency and bottlenecks in distributed systems.

---

# Interview Question

## What are the Three Pillars of Observability?

### Answer

The three pillars of observability are Metrics, Logs, and Traces. Together they provide visibility into system performance, behavior, and request flow.

---

# Interview Question

## Why are Traces Important in Microservices?

### Answer

Traces allow engineers to follow a request across multiple services and quickly identify bottlenecks, failures, and latency issues in distributed systems.

---

# Interview Question

## What is OpenTelemetry?

### Answer

OpenTelemetry is an open-source observability framework used to collect, process, and export metrics, logs, and traces from applications.
